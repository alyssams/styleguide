Typography

The type for lexus.com was carefully chosen to be strong, elegant and easy to read. Please use special consideration when laying out headlines, copy, labels and tags to keep all the pages looking consistent throughout.

Font Families

All the text on lexus.com is set in either Nobel, Pakt or Palatino. If there is any confusion about what type to use, base your choice on the PSD page.

Nobel Font

Nobel is used primarily in upper case for section headers and set in Bold, Book and Regular, but it’s also occasionally used in Book Italics. For example: Testimonials and Quotes are set in Book Italics using sentence case.

Pakt Font

All Caps VS Sentence Case

Lexus.com uses text set in all caps extensively. Here’s where you’ll normally see it used:

  • Buttons and Links
  • Navigation
  • Product Names
  • Page Headers
  • Sections headers and Subheaders
  • Prices, Months, Mileage

There are several places where all caps should not be used. Basically, anywhere the user needs to read important content or quickly scan a large amount of it. In these instances, Sentence Case should be used:

  • Article Content
  • Quotes and Testimonials
  • Vehicle Descriptions
  • Body Copy
  • Intro Paragraphs
  • Swatch Labels
  • Tables of Content

Type Vertical Spacing

Vertical spacing of type on lexus.com is also very critical. Visually, most type is given leading equal or similar to its height. Type protection from page elements or navigation can be referenced in the Desktop Standards: Vertical protection section of this Style Guide.

All the measurements for vertical spacing are taken between or from base and cap lines.

Logos

The minimum size for the Master Logo is 17.5 mm. The clearance zone is indicated by the measurement of the “L” taken from the logotype. This space is applied to ensure the integrity and readability of the logo. Apply these same rules when using the 3-D logo, while also keeping the logo on a white background. It’s important to note that the logo should not be altered (bent, rotated, outlined, etc.) in any way.

Model Tags

Model Tags are used frequently but are nonstandard elements. Each one is slightly different, but they should all have the same effect. The smaller text normally reads “All-New” or “The 2016” (depending on the model year), and the large text is the model type. With models that have more than two letters, the size and spacing can change as long as the general layout and look remain consistent. And if it’s a Hybrid model, place “Hybrid” underneath the double rule lines in the same weight and height as the smaller text.

Type Treatment

  • Nobel / bold / 12pt / #FFFFFF
  • Nobel / light / 84pt / #FFFFFF

Recalls

The Recall bar generally appears in black on the homepage and shows up either at the top or bottom of the page.

Footer

The footer is divided into six sections: Information, Shopping Tools, Services, Additional Sites and Social Media. The footer sticks to the bottom of every page and includes any disclosures and legal information. The dark background runs across the full width of the page.

Layout

The following is meant to demonstrate how different pages can look on lexus.com, showing you where the opportunities are as well as the limitations. The whole site was designed to be modular to allow for the most flexibility.

Responsive Design

Lexus.com was designed to work optimally on all screen sizes. There are four main breaking points for the site: 1204, 960, 720 and 280. Within these breakpoints there are thoughtful changes to keep the site’s look and feel consistent.

The homepage above is a great example of how our responsive layouts keep everything looking sharp while also providing the best user experience.

Destop Content Grid

All pages on Lexus.com lie on a 1032px grid with 1247px columns with 36px gutters. The grid should be center- aligned with equal spacing on the left and right

Some scenarios require a layout that does not fall exactly on the grid. While exceptions can be made, designers and developers should strive to limit how many times this happens.

Tablet Content Grid

Lexus.com in portrait view on a tablet is also set on a 12-column grid. Each column width is 38 pixels with margin sizes of 24 pixels. Fixed margins of 24 pixels are assigned to the outside of the grid.

Mobile Content Grid

Lexus.com on mobile is set on a 6-column grid with 30-pixel column width and 20-pixel gutter width.

Desktop Standards: Images
Product Image Styles

For lexus.com to achieve its robust visual style, photography shot on white is used exclusively. Shooting on any any other color should be avoided. It’s also critical that the white behind the product images is a white that does not contain washed out backgrounds or any vignetting. This ensures the vehicles remain uniform when placed in a grid or module together and that they seamlessly integrate with other white backgrounds on the site.

7/8 Vehicle Rotation

Because of the different image sizes and aspect ratios of vehicle photography, it’s often necessary to scale certain items.

Vehicles can be different sizes, shapes and colors, but they all need to fit nicely in a grid together. When different models are shown in a grid or list, it’s important that they’re all at the same size and same angle. A 7/8 angle is used to uniformly show all the models in a flattering way.

Responsive Image Scaling and Cropping

Lexus.com uses a few different responsive techniques for images. In most cases, the images simply scale at 1204px, 960px, 720px and 280px break points. To achieve the same visual effects from small to large, some elements end up being cropped on small devices.

Product Images

Lexus.com uses product images in several places throughout the site – All Models, Model Overview, Compare Landing and Side-by-Side Compare, Offers pages and in the Global Navigation. Even though models can be at different angles, they are always presented as a standard JPG on a white background. The Large version for each is shown here, and they would simply scale down for Medium and Small.

Standard 4:3 Editorial Imagery

Editorial imagery is usually presented in a 4:3 aspect ratio. It’s used consistently throughout lexus.com, especially on the Performance, Technology and Key Features pages. The large editorial images scale down for Medium and Small. This category also contains nonstandard image sizes and aspect ratios, which are detailed later in this style guide.

4:3 Thumbnails

Thumbnails, a JPEG with a 4:3 aspect ratio, can be found on the All Models, Media Gallery, Model Detail and Key Features, and Packages pages.

16:9 & 4:3 Overlays

Overlays are traditionally full-bleed images appearing on a hover or click-thru pages such as Model Overview, Media Gallery and Key Features. Both a 4:3 and 16:9 aspect ratio can be used. A 16:9 aspect ratio is preferred for Model Overview and Key Features pages. A 4:3 aspect ratio is preferred for the Media Gallery.

Nonstandard Editorial Imagery

Lexus.com sometimes contains imagery that does not fit exactly into outlined image guidelines, which usually occurs on PNGs and hero images.

"Outside the Grid" Imagery

Sometimes, lexus.com contains imagery with elements that appear to be breaking out of the image itself.

In A, the car appears to break free from its original background. It makes the background image and the negative white space of the site more prominent.

In B, the car appears to have driven out of the video that plays behind it. This creates depth and movement on the page.

In C, the vehicle pops out from the bottom. Be careful with some of these scenarios as oftentimes the bottom shadow can appear unnatural and not show the vehicle in the best light.

PNGs

PNGs allow lexus.com to be more dramatic. PNGs add depth to what would otherwise be a flat layout, letting mages expand or contract with the page itself. They have transparent areas that allow them to overlap other images in an interesting way and help push the idea of “Breaking the Grid.”

Hero Image Selection

Image is everything. So for page headers, always choose the image that makes the vehicle look the most heroic. It should give the viewer the best look at the vehicle with just a quick glance. In most cases, this will be a 3/4-front shot. But in some instances, it could be a rear shot like this one with the RC that accentuates the rear spoiler and exhaust.

Hero Image Style

Heroes on lexus.com need to accomplish two main tasks. First, they must convey a story as simply and beautifully as they can. Secondly, they establish the overall tone of the page. Typography and imagery treatments work together to create a unique feel for each page. For example: the difference between the Specs and Offers page (see above).

Full-Width Hero Images

Full-width heroes on lexus.com scale with the width of the page. They create a powerful beginning to each new page.

Cropped Hero Images

Lexus.com uses cropped heroes in a few instances to “Go outside the grid.” They can be found on pages within the Features section in the secondary navigation and sometimes when a Model Badge is present. They can also be used for section heroes that are not the heroes of the entire page.

Image Selection Guidelines

Images for Lexus should always be authentic and aspirational while conveying a sense of confidence. To find the perfect image, contact your project manager or producer to set you up with DAM (Digital Asset Management), where a library of Lexus-approved images is housed. For presentation layouts, it’s acceptable to obtain images from each vehicle’s Gallery section on lexus.com. Here are some simple guidelines to consider when choosing your shots.

Location Shots

Always look for believable situations—for images that look like they’re captured in the moment. Shots with natural light will always look the best. And be sure to keep the car angles aggressive and powerful.

Studio Shots

Select shots in real environments, not contrived sets. They should not appear slick or overly retouched. Always strive for simple and graphic compositions and a muted, sophisticated color palette.

Motion Shots

To give the best sense of motion, the backgrounds should have some movement and blur to them. Don’t use shots that are overly retouched. You can allow for reasonable reflections and imperfections.

People

Look for regionally diverse talent. They should be interacting with each other naturally, not looking at the camera or posing. Make sure a highly curated wardrobe and location are represented as well.

Interiors

The interior of the vehicles shows best in a natural light with real backgrounds to provide context. Look for dramatic, directional light to give everything a rich, sculptural look with selective focus to add depth and intrigue.

Details

Keep your close-up shots in believable, natural environments. They shouldn’t be overly retouched. Choose shots with selective focus for more depth that look as if they were captured on the fly.

Videos

Videos are found in four areas of the site: Homepage, Feature Overlays, the Gallery Page and the Model Overview Pages. They are all treated with a play icon. Video thumbnails exist as a standard editorial image. And if a video is played in an overlay, i.e., in The Gallery, a close button is placed on the top right to collapse the video.
