Typography

The type for lexus.com was carefully chosen to be strong, elegant and easy to read. Please use special consideration when laying out headlines, copy, labels and tags to keep all the pages looking consistent throughout.

Font Families

All the text on lexus.com is set in either Nobel, Pakt or Palatino. If there is any confusion about what type to use, base your choice on the PSD page.

Nobel Font

Nobel is used primarily in upper case for section headers and set in Bold, Book and Regular, but it’s also occasionally used in Book Italics. For example: Testimonials and Quotes are set in Book Italics using sentence case.

Pakt Font

Pakt is generally used for Display text, and numbers in Bold and Semibold. It’s a tall, condensed font that really stands out for dramatic effect. When a character other than a number is needed (i.e., $,%, etc.), that character should be set at about 1/2 the size.

Layout

The following is meant to demonstrate how different pages can look on lexus.com, showing you where the opportunities are as well as the limitations. The whole site was designed to be modular to allow for the most flexibility.

Responsive Design

Lexus.com was designed to work optimally on all screen sizes. There are four main breaking points for the site: 1204, 960, 640 and 320. Within these breakpoints there are thoughtful changes to keep the site’s look and feel consistent.

Imagery

The key attributes of Lexus imagery are its emotion, humanity and authenticity. In the following, you’ll see examples of the desired photography and how it’s used. Keep in mind, however, since each model or campaign has a different strategic point of view, you can allow for variations along the way.

Backgrounds - Gray & White Variation

Lexus.com uses a banded design that relies on white and occasionally gray backgrounds. Most modules are on white backgrounds only. Gray is used occasionally expanding modules to help organize large amounts of information.

Desktop Standards: Images

Product Image Styles

For lexus.com to achieve its robust visual style, photography shot on white is used exclusively. Shooting on any any other color should be avoided. It’s also critical that the white behind the product images is a white that does not contain washed out backgrounds or any vignetting. This ensures the vehicles remain uniform when placed in a grid or module together and that they seamlessly integrate with other white backgrounds on the site.

7/8 Vehicle Rotation

Because of the different image sizes and aspect ratios of vehicle photography, it’s often necessary to scale certain items.

Vehicles can be different sizes, shapes and colors, but they all need to fit nicely in a grid together. When different models are shown in a grid or list, it’s important that they’re all at the same size and same angle. A 7/8 angle is used to uniformly show all the models in a flattering way.

Responsive Image Scaling and Cropping

Lexus.com uses a few different responsive techniques for images. In most cases, the images simply scale at 1204px, 960px, 720px and 280px break points. To achieve the same visual effects from small to large, some elements end up being cropped on small devices.

Standard 4:3 Editorial Imagery

Editorial imagery is usually presented in a 4:3 aspect ratio. It’s used consistently throughout lexus.com, especially on the Performance, Technology and Key Features pages. The large editorial images scale down for Medium and Small. This category also contains nonstandard image sizes and aspect ratios, which are detailed later in this style guide.

4:3 Thumbnails

Thumbnails, a JPEG with a 4:3 aspect ratio, can be found on the All Models, Media Gallery, Model Detail and Key Features, and Packages pages.

16:9 & 4:3 Overlays

Overlays are traditionally full-bleed images appearing on a hover or click-thru pages such as Model Overview, Media Gallery and Key Features. Both a 4:3 and 16:9 aspect ratio can be used. A 16:9 aspect ratio is preferred for Model Overview and Key Features pages. A 4:3 aspect ratio is preferred for the Media Gallery.

Location Shots

Always look for believable situations—for images that look like they’re captured in the moment. Shots with natural light will always look the best. And be sure to keep the car angles aggressive and powerful.

Image Details
  • BELIEVABLE SITUATIONS, NOT CONTRIVED
  • IMAGES LOOK LIKE THEY’RE CAPTURED IN THE MOMENT, NOT SET UP
  • AVAILABLE OR NATURAL LIGHT
  • NOT OVERLY RETOUCHED
  • SMALLER-FORMAT HANDHELD CAMERA WHEN POSSIBLE
  • MUTED YET SOPHISTICATED TONES
  • GRAPHIC AND SIMPLE COMPOSITIONS
  • HIGHLY CURATED WARDROBE AND LOCATION ARCHITECTURE
  • SELECTIVE FOCUS ADDS DEPTH AND INTRIGUE
  • CAR ANGLES MUST BE AGGRESSIVE AND POWERFUL
  • ALLOW REASONABLE REFLECTIONS AND IMPERFECTIONS

Select shots in real environments, not contrived sets. They should not appear slick or overly retouched. Always strive for simple and graphic compositions and a muted, sophisticated color palette.

Motion Shots

To give the best sense of motion, the backgrounds should have some movement and blur to them. Don’t use shots that are overly retouched. You can allow for reasonable reflections and imperfections.

Interiors

The interior of the vehicles shows best in a natural light with real backgrounds to provide context. Look for dramatic, directional light to give everything a rich, sculptural look with selective focus to add depth and intrigue.

Image Details
